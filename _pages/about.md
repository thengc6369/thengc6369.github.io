I am a planetary atmospheric scientist studying various climate phenomena on Earth and Mars. 

On Earth, my focus has been on the dynamics of stratospheric polar vortices, including their breakdown, stratospheric sudden warmings.

On Mars, I have investigated various components of the planet's climate. My analyses have targeted polar vortex oscillation cycles, midlatitude baroclinic wave amplifications, regional-scale dust storm formation, thermal tide reconstructions from surface lander observations, and tropical cloud belt variability.

My research uses a wide selection of methods, ranging from reduced theoretical models where analytic progress is achiveable to numerical climate model simulations and to statistical analyses of observational data.
